Help & how it works

Inside Rank is a compare-first SEO dashboard for the InsideCasino affiliate review network. It reads Google Search Console exports and surfaces real ranking movement — including positions 31–100 that Ahrefs marks as "lost".

📋 1. Pick a site
The sidebar has a site switcher for the three properties: insidecasino.ca (Canada), insidecasino.co.nz (New Zealand), and insidecasino.com (UK). All snapshots and comparisons are scoped to the active site. Switching sites resets the Period A / B selection.
📤 2. How to export & import data
You need one ZIP file per reporting period. Each ZIP becomes one snapshot. To compare two periods you need two ZIPs.
How to export a ZIP from Google Search Console
  1. Go to Google Search Console → Performance → Search results
  2. Set your date range — e.g. "Feb 16, 2026 → May 15, 2026" using the Custom option
  3. Make sure no filters are active — no Country, Device, or Search type filter. Any active filter carries into the export and will make KPI totals differ from the GSC header.
  4. Click Export → Download CSV — this downloads a .zip file
  5. Repeat for your second period — e.g. "Nov 16, 2025 → Feb 15, 2026"
  6. Go to Imports and drop both ZIPs at the same time — two snapshots are created automatically
What's inside the ZIP
The ZIP contains several CSVs. Inside Rank reads each one automatically:
  • Queries.csv — top ~1,000 keywords with position, clicks, impressions, CTR. Powers the Keywords tab and position comparisons.
  • Pages.csv — top landing pages with aggregate metrics. Powers the Pages tab and URL-to-keyword matching.
  • Chart.csv — daily totals across all keywords (not capped at 1,000). Used for the Overview KPI cards so they match the GSC UI exactly.
  • Countries.csv, Devices.csv, Filters.csv, Search appearance.csv — skipped automatically.
Always use the full ZIP, not individual CSVs. Individual CSV exports only contain the Queries tab, capped at 1,000 keywords. The ZIP includes Chart.csv which is the only way to get totals that match the GSC Performance dashboard.
🔄 3. Compare two snapshots
The header bar lets you pick Period A (baseline / older) and Period B (current / newer). Every tab reflects this A vs B comparison. The two newest snapshots are auto-selected when you first arrive. Click either picker to change the selection.
📈 4. Reading the dashboard
  • Overview — KPI cards (Clicks, Impressions, Avg Position, Top 3, Pos 11–30, Pos 31–100), position distribution A vs B, health summary, top winners and losers, and top 10 keywords by clicks and impressions.
  • Keywords — every keyword with Position A→B, Δ POS, Clicks, Impressions, CTR, status badge, and URL. Filter by status, position range, or hide rows without a URL. The scrollbar at the top of the table lets you scroll right without going to the bottom of the page.
  • Pages — all landing pages grouped by category. Each row shows keyword count, improved/declined/new/lost counts, and metrics A→B. Expand any row to see every keyword that page ranks for.
🔢 Why the numbers might look different from GSC
The most common reason is comparing the dashboard (one period) against the GSC UI showing a different, combined date range.
Real example:
 • Period A: Nov 16, 2025 → Feb 15, 2026 = 305 clicks, 732K impressions
 • Period B: Feb 16, 2026 → May 15, 2026 = 226 clicks, 1.16M impressions

The dashboard shows Period B: 226 clicks · 1.16M impressions · 55.3 avg pos

If you open GSC and set the date to Nov 16, 2025 → May 15, 2026 (both periods combined), GSC shows 531 clicks and 1.9M impressions.

305 + 226 = 531 ✓   732K + 1.16M ≈ 1.9M ✓ — the numbers are correct, they just cover different date ranges.
To verify: In GSC, set the date range to the exact same period as your snapshot (e.g. Feb 16 → May 15 only). The numbers will match.
Other causes of small differences:
  • A filter was active in GSC when you exported (Country / Device / Search type). Always export with no filters applied.
  • GSC data is updated up to 48 hours after the fact — numbers can shift slightly between export and when you check the UI.
📖 Why GSC instead of Ahrefs?
Ahrefs only crawls the top ~30 search results, so anything ranking 31–100 shows as "Lost" — even though you're still ranking. GSC reports your actual impressions and average position across the entire SERP. Use the Pos 31–100 KPI card and the 31–100 (Ahrefs blind) filter in Keywords to see everything Ahrefs misses.
💾 Data storage & sharing
All data is stored privately in your browser — nothing is sent to a server or shared between team members. Each person on the team imports their own ZIPs independently. To reset, go to Imports → Clear all.
